Rank & Role Structure
The game provides 5 ranks (R1–R5). Top alliances layer functional roles on top of these to define who owns what operationally.
R5 Alliance Leader
1 seat
  • Sole authority to edit alliance profile, appoint/demote officers, demolish buildings, and disband
  • Sets strategic direction — war targets, diplomacy decisions, KvK alignment
  • Final arbiter on member disputes and alliance policy
  • Maintains relationships with allied R5s and kingdom-level diplomats
  • Should designate a named Acting R5 for planned absences over 48 hours
Leadership Transfer Protocol: The R5 seat can be transferred in-game at any time. Top alliances establish a succession order — designate your #1 and #2 trusted R4s who would receive the seat in an emergency. Never leave the alliance without a human holding R5.
R4 Officer (Cabinet)
4–8 seats

R4s are the operational core. Each should own a functional domain below — avoid spreading any single R4 too thin.

🗡 Warlord / War Officer
  • Calls and leads rallies
  • Coordinates troop marches during Holy Site captures
  • Manages war phase messaging and rally timing
  • Tracks enemy power and flag positioning
📜 Counsellor / Ops Officer
  • Manages tech donation drives
  • Schedules and cancels alliance events
  • Territory expansion — flags, fortress placement
  • Resource center rotation
🌐 Diplomat
  • Maintains NAP list and ally contacts
  • Handles kingdom chat and inter-alliance comms
  • Manages family/sub-alliance coordination
  • Negotiates ceasefires and agreements
👥 Recruitment Officer
  • Reviews and approves membership applications
  • Posts recruitment in kingdom chat and forums
  • Conducts power/activity vetting
  • Manages member probation and removal
Game mechanic: R4 Warlord title gives +1% troop ATK/DEF. Counsellor title gives +1% research and build speed. Assign titles to match each officer's actual function.
R3 Senior Member / Squad Lead
Unlimited
  • Trusted active players, battlefield leaders, and mentors to newer members
  • Can send alliance-wide messages — use sparingly and purposefully
  • No kick/promote privileges — escalate to R4 for personnel actions
  • Ideal for timezone leads who cover shifts R4s can't
R2 Member
  • Standard alliance member — full chat, donation, event participation
  • Cannot send alliance-wide messages
  • Elevation to R3 is a merit-based recognition milestone
R1 Recruit / Probationary
30-day window
  • New joins start here — observe activity and contribution before promoting
  • Can use alliance chat and help buttons but no broadcast capability
  • Promote to R2 after 2–4 weeks of consistent activity and tech donations
  • R1s inactive for 14+ days are candidates for removal (enforce consistently)
Officer Duty Standards
What "being an R4" actually means day-to-day. These are baseline expectations — customize to your alliance's activity level.
The #1 alliance killer: An R4 who is offline during every war phase. Before promoting anyone to officer, confirm their timezone and confirm they can be reachable during your critical windows (Holy Site defense, KvK war phases, fort rallies).
Daily Baseline (All R4s)
  • Log into the alliance at least once per day during active phases
  • Donate to alliance tech on login (Development tree first, then War tree)
  • Check Discord/comms for outstanding decisions or alerts
  • Review member application queue if you are the Recruitment Officer
  • Repair any burning flags or fortresses immediately upon login
  • Post coverage notice in Discord if you'll be unavailable for 24+ hours
War Officer Specific — During War Phases
  • Post rally call in Discord with target coordinates, troop type, and march time
  • Coordinate who is leading the rally (highest power commander)
  • Confirm minimum troop fill before launching (establish your threshold, e.g., 70%)
  • Post BVB/KvK march orders with clear "march time" so all timezones align
  • Debrief after major engagements — what worked, what didn't
Ops Officer Specific
  • Keep resource center active and rotated weekly
  • Schedule events (Barbarian Fort rallies, ARK, MGE windows) in advance
  • Monitor flag chain integrity — check for gaps that make flags ineffective
  • Maintain a territory expansion plan — don't build flags randomly
  • Track tech donation leaders and give public recognition monthly
R4 Succession & Rotation
  • Designate a primary and backup for each functional role — no single points of failure
  • R4s who are inactive for 7+ days without notice move to R3 temporarily
  • Re-evaluate R4 roster every KvK cycle — performance during war phases is the main criterion
  • R5 should hold a brief leadership debrief after every major event (KvK, Holy Site siege)
  • Avoid "legacy R4s" — officers who are promoted and never replaced even after going inactive
Timezone Coverage Framework
The biggest tactical vulnerability most alliances have is coverage gaps. Map your officer team to shifts before you have a crisis.
First step: Survey every R4 and R3 for their timezone and typical active hours. Build the shift map below with real names before KvK starts.

US East reference: EST = UTC-5 / EDT = UTC-4. A 6:00 PM EDT event is 22:00 UTC. Your prime time (evening ET) falls in Delta Shift.
Coverage Principles
  • Schedule Holy Site captures and war marches during your highest-coverage window (Charlie or Delta for US-heavy alliances)
  • If you have zero R4s online during a shift, assign at least one R3 as "shift lead" with authority to call for help
  • Use Discord pings with timezone-specific roles (e.g., @EU-Online, @NA-Online) to reach the right people fast
  • Never schedule a flagship rally during a known dead zone — enemies will time attacks to hit you there
  • ARK (Alliance Resource Center) events should be announced 12+ hours ahead to reach all timezones

Events & Coordination Calendar
Key recurring event types and how top alliances structure them. Build your actual calendar in Discord or a shared doc.
🏰 Barbarian Fort Rallies
  • Level 10 forts are the optimal AP-to-Honor-Point trade — prioritize these
  • Schedule 2–3 times per week at consistent times so members can plan around them
  • War Officer announces target 30 min ahead with coordinates in Discord
  • Assign a secondary rally leader as backup in case primary is offline
  • Post "AP refill reminder" in chat before planned rally windows
⚡ MGE (More Than Gems)
  • Coordinate with full alliance 48 hours before MGE opens
  • Announce which stages align with current kingdom objectives
  • Remind members to stockpile relevant resources/speedups before the event
  • Post a leaderboard update mid-event to motivate participation
🌾 ARK (Alliance Resource Center)
  • Ops Officer rotates resource type weekly based on tech donation needs
  • Announce resource type switch 6+ hours before changeover
  • Announce open gather window — members queue gatherers immediately
  • Never leave ARK empty — always have the next type pre-planned
🏛 Holy Site Operations
  • Never attempt to capture unplanned — brief the alliance in Discord first
  • Assign garrison commanders before the capture rally lands
  • Post "hold the site" messaging — everyone knows to reinforce, not recall
  • War Officer stays online for 2+ hours post-capture to manage counterattacks

KvK Readiness Framework
How competitive alliances prepare for Kingdom vs. Kingdom — the event that separates organized alliances from power numbers on a screen.
The core truth: KvK is won by the most organized alliance, not the highest power. A 60-member alliance that executes together consistently beats a 90-member alliance doing 90 different things.
Pre-KvK Preparation (4–6 Weeks Out)
  • 1
    Power Audit

    Document each member's power, primary troop type (Cav/Inf/Arch), march capacity, and top commanders. Know your actual war capability before day one.

  • 2
    Tech Push

    All-hands donation drive to max out War tech tree nodes that affect troop ATK and rally capacity. This is the single highest ROI preparation activity.

  • 3
    Stockpile Teleports

    Every active war member needs Targeted Teleports stocked. The Alliance Shop should be draining teleports weekly. War is lost when players can't move to the fight.

  • 4
    Diplomatic Alignment

    Finalize NAP and ally list before KvK opens. Know who you fight with and who you don't touch. Ambiguity during war phases causes friendly fire incidents that break alliances.

  • 5
    War Officer Drills

    Run at least 2 coordinated fort rallies under real war conditions — timed march calls, Discord voice coordination, debrief. Drills expose gaps before they matter.

During KvK — Operational Rules
RuleRationale
No solo attacks on designated targets Coordinated rallies hit harder and safer. Uncoordinated attacks give enemies time to reinforce.
Shields up during offline hours High-power members who sleep with shields down become priority targets. Enforce this with an inactivity warning protocol.
All march calls go through Discord In-game chat is chaotic during KvK. Discord war-orders channel is the command channel — if it's not there, it doesn't happen.
Confirm troop counts before launch A rally launching at 40% fill is a losing rally. Establish and enforce a minimum fill threshold.
No gathering during active war phases Gathered troops are exposed. War phase = troops inside your city or marching to a fight.

[ YOUR ALLIANCE NAME ] — Community Rules

Kingdom 4054 · Est. [ Date ]

We are building one of the strongest alliances in Kingdom 4054. These rules exist to protect the investment every member makes in this alliance. They apply equally to all ranks.

  1. Be active. Minimum login once every 48 hours during KvK, once every 5 days during peacetime. Members inactive for 7 days without notice will be removed to maintain alliance roster health.
  2. Donate to tech on every login. Tech donations benefit everyone. It is the single lowest-effort, highest-impact action you take each day.
  3. Honor all NAPs and ally designations. Do not attack, farm, or reinforce against alliances on our protected list. Violations are an immediate removal offense.
  4. Follow war orders from War Officers and R5. During KvK and Holy Site operations, the march call in Discord is the command. No independent actions without leadership approval.
  5. Use Discord. Critical communications happen in Discord. Not being on Discord during war phases is not an excuse for missing march calls or rally coordination.
  6. No drama. Disputes go to R4 via DM, not in alliance chat. Public arguments, insults, or bad-faith behavior toward alliance members result in removal.
  7. Flag placement requires approval. Do not build flags or fortresses without R4/R5 direction. Unauthorized expansion can trigger diplomatic incidents.
  8. Farm accounts must be in designated farm alliances. No farm accounts in the main alliance roster. Use [ FARM ALLIANCE NAME ] for that purpose.
ENFORCEMENT

First offense: Warning from R4 via Discord DM.
Second offense: Demotion to R1 / probationary status for 7 days.
Third offense or severe violations (NAP breaks, solo war attacks): Immediate removal.
